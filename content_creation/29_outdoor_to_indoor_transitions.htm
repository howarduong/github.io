Creating an entrance/exit

To create an entrance/exit in WorldEditor, follow the steps below:

1 Open WorldEditor.

2 To create a seamless entryway between an outdoor space and an indoor shell, first select an appropriate location for your entrance.

If you have not created any area for this, it is suggested that you create a gentle hillside in the terrain, in order to have an area that an entrance model can be moulded into.

To make future additions to the interior shell set easy, do the following:

Face the entrance/exit either north, south, east, or west.

Select the Object panel's Object Grid Snaps group box's Enabled check box, then click its Shell Snaps button – this will set the x, y, z, and Angle field to optimal values for shell placement.

3 Now that we have selected an area that is appropriate for an entrance/exit, we should place and position our entrance/exit model.

For the purposes of this tutorial, we use one entrance/exit model, one entrance/exit shell and one interior shell.

4 Position your entrance/exit into the hillside itself.

You should try to 'bury' it into the terrain, as you will later cut away the overlapping terrain sections.

Cutting the Terrain

This section describes how to cut the terrain as part of creating an entrance/exit in your world.

1 Select the Terrain Mesh Tool () toolbar button – the Terrain Mesh Cut/Repair panel will be activated.

2 Select the Brush Mode group box's Cut option button.

3 Set the Size field to 1.

4 Zoom into your entrance/exit model for a better view of the areas of terrain that you will need to cut.

5 Click the Pole Markers () toolbar button – this will activate the Pole Markers chunk visualisation mode.

6 Cut the terrain by dragging the mouse over the areas that are blocking the entrance/exit. It is important to leave room for reshaping the terrain around your entrance/exit model after this cutting process.

After this step, you will now notice large holes in the terrain from certain angles.

7 Click the Terrain Height Tool () toolbar button – the Terrain Height panel will be activated.

8 Gently reshape the terrain to fit perfectly around this model.

This is the most difficult and time consuming step in the entire process, as terrain heights will differ from model to model. It might take a few minutes of massaging the terrain into the correct shapes, and you might even have to reposition the entrance/exit.

To help with areas in this terrain blending, try placing objects such as plants, etc., as it would also be a natural area for these models to exist, and where they help to draw the eye away from the transition area.

Placing an interior shell

This section describes how to place your interior shell (which must contain an exit portal) as part of creating an entrance/exit in your world.

If you do not have a shell with an exit portal, then see the lesson Create an Internal Shell.

1 Click the Object Tool () toolbar button – the Object panel will be activated.

2 In the Asset Browser panel, select the Shells virtual folder.

3 Drag the desired shell from the assets list to the viewport.

4 Ensure that your exit portal (B) begins before the overlapping terrain cut (A) (see diagram 1). Otherwise the player will not be able to enter the exit portal and will instead collide with the terrain and be lifted to the surface as in diagram 2.

5 Connect all your interior shells to this entry/exit portal. Shells can be snapped into place using the portal resolving feature – simply select your shell and middle-click over a shell you would like it to snap to.

You can continue to build your interior spaces inward from this entrance.

Another method of transition form indoor to outdoor space is to simply use additional models to surround your entrance/exit. This method does not require you to cut into any terrain segments, and may be useful in some, yet not all situations. Be creative with your entrances – this is a great example of the use of additional objects to turn the entrance into a feature, and blend it perfectly with the scenery, without cutting away terrain.

For objects that act as a façade to internal entrances, it might be necessary to turn the WorldEditor's editable flag Outside Only to True in the Properties panel. This prevents the façade from being rendered as if it were inside the internal chunk. For more details, see the lesson Create Internal Shells.
